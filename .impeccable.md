## Design Context

### Users

People using a **digital journal prototype** for structured self-reflection (Best Version of Me). Context is typically quiet, personal, and screen-based—clarity and calm matter more than density.

### Brand Personality

**Reflective · gentle · trustworthy** — a guided worksheet feel, not a dashboard or exam.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Light mode**, **blue–grey** worksheet palette (defined in Tailwind `bvm.*` tokens); no pivot to purple gradients or high-contrast neon.
- **Typography**: **Inter** for UI and body; **Lora** for page and section titles only—editorial separation without clutter.
- **Anti-patterns for this project**: loud marketing layouts, heavy shadows, “card soup,” or generic AI purple-on-white.

### Design Principles

1. **Hierarchy first**: title → section → labels → controls → meta; one clear step between each level.
2. **Breathing room**: generous vertical rhythm between blocks; subtle dividers, not harsh grid lines.
3. **Readable at rest**: comfortable line-height on prompts and supporting copy; balanced headings on short lines (`text-balance` where helpful).
4. **Preserve intent**: rating control variety stays for comparison; polish around them, don’t replace them.
